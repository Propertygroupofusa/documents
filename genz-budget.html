Screen 01
Splash — Lead with identity
Zero friction. Name + tagline + one CTA. No email required yet. Trust is earned before data is collected. "money that moves with you" — positions app as adaptive, not restrictive
Screen 02
Vibe Check — Personalize fast
3-step onboarding max. Income situation drives the entire default setup. "No judgment" copy reduces anxiety about financial disclosure. "It's complicated" option validates messy realities Gen Z actually lives
Screen 03
Home — One number dominates
"Left to spend" is the hero metric — not total balance. Gen Z needs permission to spend, not just tracking of what's gone. "You're looking decent" — affirming, not clinical
Screen 04–06
Spend → Goals → Nudge
Each screen deepens commitment. Spend shows reality. Goals create aspiration. Nudge closes the loop with actionable micro-decisions — never lectures. AI nudge offers a choice, never a command

04 — Quick Concept Models
Three Ideas to Explore
Concept A
Social Budgeting
Spend tracking that's aware of your social context — splitting bills, shared goals with friends, group savings.
👥
Group Goals Friends pool money toward a shared trip, concert, or event. Everyone sees progress in real time.
⚡
Instant Split Dinner bill appears in app. One tap sends your share. No Venmo context-switching.
🏆
Streak Battles Challenge a friend to a no-spend weekend. Loser buys coffee.
Concept B
Mood-Based Spending
Tag purchases by mood at checkout. Discover patterns between emotional states and spending behavior.
😤
Mood Tagging "How were you feeling?" appears after each transaction. Takes 1 second.
📊
Pattern Reports "You spend 3x more when stressed. Last Tuesday: $84 after a bad shift."
🧘
Pause Prompts When a purchase matches a "stress pattern," app offers a 30-second pause before confirming.
Concept C
Income-First Design
Built for irregular income. Gig workers, freelancers, and students whose money doesn't arrive on schedule.
🌊
Flow Mode Budget adapts in real time when a new paycheck, tip, or gig payment lands.
📅
No Monthly Cycle Budget spans from "last paycheck" to "next expected paycheck" — not Jan 1 to 31.
🛡️
Dry Spell Buffer App auto-identifies low-income periods and tightens spending limits preemptively.
