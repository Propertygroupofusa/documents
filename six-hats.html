What the data tells us without interpretation

The referral infrastructure is built and operational at the database level. A worker can generate a referral code, share it manually, and a new worker can use it at signup. The payout record is created. What is missing is every layer between the code existing and a worker actually using it: no in-product prompting, no share mechanism, no automated reward, no notification to the referrer when their code is used.

The program as currently designed requires a worker to (a) know their code exists, (b) remember it unprompted, (c) share it through their own channels with no platform support, and (d) wait for a manual payout with no ETA communicated. The activation funnel has four unbuilt steps.

The emotional pattern underneath the data

The dominant emotion across all personas is distrust born from silence. Not hostility — just the quiet suspicion that forms when a promised reward produces no confirmation, no timeline, and no proof it's real. Workers who would be natural advocates are holding back because they won't stake their personal credibility on a platform reward system they can't verify.

The second emotional signal is untapped pride. Workers who feel genuinely good about the platform — who believe they're earning fairly and being treated like professionals — want to tell people. That impulse exists. What's missing is a structure that makes sharing feel easy, trustworthy, and worth their while. Referral programs don't create advocates. They give existing advocates a mechanism and a reason.

The structural vulnerability no one is talking about

The highest-severity risk isn't fraud — it's the trust collapse from manual payouts. When a worker shares their referral code and nothing visibly happens afterward, they experience the platform as untrustworthy. This poisons not just the referral program but their entire relationship with PGUSA. They become less likely to claim jobs, less likely to recommend the platform, and more likely to churn. A broken referral promise costs far more than $25.

The fraud risk is secondary but real. Without a verified signup requirement (W-9 submission, first job completion, or ID verification), the $25 bonus is exploitable by anyone who can create email accounts. Cap the exposure now by changing the trigger before the program is actively promoted.

Network Effect Opportunity
Notary communities are tight-knit
Notaries, tax preparers, and legal doc workers organize in professional associations, Facebook groups, and NNA forums. One highly-regarded referrer in those communities can bring dozens of qualified workers.
Quality Advantage
Referred workers are pre-screened socially
A notary who refers a colleague has already done a quality filter — they won't stake their own reputation on someone they don't trust. Referral = social vetting that costs the platform nothing.
Geographic Expansion
Workers naturally refer in their own market
A Dallas notary refers Dallas contacts. A Houston tax preparer brings Houston workers. Referral is the lowest-cost way to build density in new cities without a city-by-city marketing budget.
Compounding Returns
Each referred worker can refer again
At a k-factor above 1.0, referred users produce more than one additional signup each. The program becomes self-sustaining and scales without additional marketing spend once the flywheel starts.

The best-case scenario is genuinely transformational

A properly structured referral program in a credentialed professional market — where trust is already a premium commodity — is one of the highest-leverage growth tools available. The credential requirement means referred workers are already filtered. The community structure means high-volume referrers exist and are identifiable. The earnings model means workers have a financial stake in the platform's success.

If PGUSA fixes the payout trigger, automates the reward, adds a share mechanism, and raises the incentive to $50 on first job completion, the program has the potential to drive 40–60% of new worker acquisition within six months — at a fraction of the cost of any paid channel.

Green Hat · Creativity & Innovation
What if we tried something completely different?
New angles, lateral thinking, unconventional structures — no judgment
01
The Crew Model — Tiered Team Bonuses
Instead of individual referrals, workers build "crews." A worker who brings in 3+ active colleagues earns crew leader status — unlocking a 5% revenue share on every job their crew completes, permanently. The referring worker becomes a micro-franchisor with ongoing income, not a one-time $25 bonus earner.
Why this works: Transforms transactional referring into entrepreneurial team-building. Workers stop sharing a code and start recruiting with the same energy they'd give their own business.
02
Referral as a Premium Service — Client-Side Program
Title companies, mortgage brokers, and real estate attorneys who refer clients to PGUSA earn a $15 credit per closed booking — not cash, but a credit toward their own PGUSA document services. The incentive is embedded in their existing workflow, and the credit keeps them coming back as clients too.
Why this works: Adds a B2B referral channel that currently doesn't exist. One title company partner who closes 50 transactions/month becomes a repeating demand source, not just a one-time referral.
03
The Reputation Multiplier — Bonus Scales With Referred Worker Quality
Base referral: $25 on first job completion. Bonus: +$25 if the referred worker earns a 5-star rating on their first job. Bonus: +$25 more if they complete 10 jobs within 60 days. Maximum referral value: $75 per high-quality worker. Workers are incentivized to refer people who are actually good, not just anyone.
Why this works: Aligns the referral incentive with platform quality. The program becomes self-filtering — workers only refer people they're confident will perform, because their own bonus depends on it.
04
The Association Play — Partner With NNA and NAEA Directly
Instead of worker-to-worker referrals, negotiate a partnership with the National Notary Association or National Association of Enrolled Agents. PGUSA becomes a featured employer/marketplace partner. NNA members get a $0 platform fee for the first 30 days. PGUSA gets access to 350,000+ credentialed notaries without a single referral code.
Why this works: Bypasses the referral problem entirely by going directly to the organized networks where credentialed workers already congregate. One partnership replaces thousands of individual referrals.
05
Referral as Content — The Shareable Earnings Card
After every payout, workers receive a shareable "earnings card" — a branded visual showing their earnings this month, their rating, and their referral code embedded as a QR code. Designed to be posted on LinkedIn, Instagram, or in notary Facebook groups. The referral code is the CTA on content the worker was already motivated to share.
Why this works: Turns every payout moment into an organic marketing event. The referral code rides on pride, not obligation. Workers share because they're proud of what they earned — the referral is just along for the ride.
06
Invert It — Let Clients Refer Workers
A client who has a great experience with their notary gets a prompt: "Know a notary as good as [worker name]? Refer them to our platform and get $20 off your next booking." Clients become worker recruiters — and their recommendation carries the weight of a satisfied customer, which is the strongest possible endorsement for a new worker to join.
Why this works: Clients know professionals in the industries they interact with. A real estate investor who just had a great closing knows five other notaries personally. That's a recruiting channel nobody is using.
Blue Hat · Process & Control
How do we run this well?
Sequencing, ownership, metrics, and the master plan — no ideas, just execution

The three non-negotiable fixes before promoting the program

  • 1. Change the payout trigger from signup to first job completed — eliminates fraud risk and aligns cost with revenue
  • 2. Automate the payout via the existing payment cron job — removes manual liability and builds trust through instant confirmation
  • 3. Add a share mechanism to the dashboard — copy-to-clipboard referral link, one-tap SMS share, and a visible bonus counter
Week 1
Foundation
Fix the trigger and automate payout
  • Update Referral bonus_paid trigger: fire on referred worker's first completed job, not signup
  • Wire payout into the existing complete_job() payment flow — create a Payment record for the referrer automatically
  • Send SMS to referrer the moment their referred worker completes job 1: "Your referral just completed their first job. $25 is on its way to you."
  • Add fraud guard: one referral bonus per unique phone number verified worker
Owner: Backend · Est. 4 hours
Week 1
Visibility
Build the share surface in the dashboard
  • Add referral widget to worker dashboard: shows code, total referrals, total earned from referrals
  • One-tap copy button for referral link (pgusa.com/join?ref=CODE)
  • Pre-written SMS message: "I've been earning on PGUSA — notary/tax prep jobs, paid every 4 days. Join with my link and we both get $25 when you complete your first job: [link]"
  • Show referral leaderboard: "3 workers joined using your code this month"
Owner: Frontend · Est. 3 hours
Week 2
Incentive
Raise the stakes and add the quality multiplier
  • Increase base referral to $50 (first job completion trigger)
  • Add quality bonus: +$25 if referred worker earns 5 stars on first job
  • Add volume bonus: +$25 if referred worker completes 10 jobs in 60 days
  • Update all dashboard copy and onboarding messaging to reflect new $50 base
Owner: Product + Backend · Est. 3 hours
Week 3
Activation
Launch the shareable earnings card
  • After each payout, generate a branded earnings summary card (HTML → image via API or canvas)
  • Card shows: worker first name, earnings this month, star rating, referral QR code
  • "Share your win" CTA with one-tap to save image + referral link
  • Post examples to TikTok and LinkedIn to seed social proof
Owner: Marketing + Dev · Est. 6 hours
Month 2
Expansion
Open the client-side referral channel
  • Build client referral flow: after job completion, prompt "Know a notary? Refer them, get $20 off your next booking"
  • Target B2B: title companies get a dedicated referral page with volume incentives
  • Identify top 3 referring workers, offer crew leader status (ongoing revenue share)
  • Approach NNA and NAEA about platform partnership listing
Owner: Del + Dev · Est. 10 hours

The metrics that tell you if it's working

  • → Referral activation rate: % of active workers who have shared their code at least once. Target: 40% within 30 days of launch
  • → Referral conversion rate: referred signups who complete a first job. Target: >50% (vs organic signup baseline)
  • → Referral CAC: total bonus paid ÷ active workers acquired. Target: under $60 per activated worker
  • → K-factor: average referrals per active referrer. Anything above 1.0 means the program is self-sustaining
  • → Referred worker 90-day retention: benchmark against organic signups. Expect 20–40% better
