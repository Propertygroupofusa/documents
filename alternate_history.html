Pricing Architecture
What if we launched with a premium-only pricing model?
+
Reality — What we built
Tiered pricing: RON at $14.99, Mobile Notary $39, Tax Prep $49–$149. Low-cost entry to capture volume and compete on price vs national platforms.
Rationale: Undercut competitors (TurboTax $300+, Notarize $25+) to attract first customers and prove demand before raising prices.
Alternate — What if
Premium positioning: Notary $89 flat, Tax Prep $149–$299, Real Estate $199. "White glove, verified professional" framing. No race to the bottom.
Premise: The market for trusted, vetted document services is inelastic at the top. Clients who need a real estate closing done right will pay $199, not $99.

02 / 05
Worker Tier Structure
What if we launched with credential-gated access from day one?
+
Reality — What we built
Three tiers (Starter free, Pro $29, Elite $79) with Starter auto-approved. Workers access jobs immediately. Credential verification is optional and async.
Rationale: Lower friction to build supply. Get workers onto the platform first, verify credentials over time as the relationship develops.
Alternate — What if
Credential-first: All workers must submit verified notary commission or PTIN before accessing any jobs. Approval is 24-hour SLA with human review. Premium positioning from launch.
Premise: Supply quality beats supply quantity. Ten verified, excellent workers fill jobs better than 100 unverified ones, and B2B clients require verified workers anyway.

03 / 05
Technology Architecture
What if we had used a no-code platform instead of FastAPI + Railway?
+
Reality — What we built
FastAPI backend on Railway, PostgreSQL, async SQLAlchemy, 8 routers, Stripe Connect, Twilio SMS, GitHub Pages frontend. Full custom stack built from scratch.
Rationale: Custom stack gives full control over worker compliance, payment flows, credential verification, and the ability to add any feature without platform limitations.
Alternate — What if
Bubble.io or Glide for frontend + backend, Stripe Checkout (not Connect) for payments, Airtable for worker database, Zapier for automation. Launched in 2 weeks instead of 8.
Premise: No-code launches faster, tests the market sooner, and defers technical debt until product-market fit is confirmed.

Go-to-Market Strategy
What if we had gone B2B-first instead of consumer worker recruiting?
+
Reality — What we built
Worker-first consumer GTM: recruit individual notaries and tax preparers via social media, Gumroad, TikTok, and direct outreach. Build supply, then attract demand.
Rationale: Supply-side marketplaces need workers before clients. Seeded with Kisha Dial as first worker. Plans to market services to individual clients through the documents frontend.
Alternate — What if
B2B-first: Sign 3 title companies and 2 mortgage brokers as anchor clients before recruiting a single worker. Guaranteed job volume attracts workers. Revenue from Day 1.
Premise: In a marketplace, demand certainty is more valuable than supply abundance. One title company closing 40 transactions/month is worth more than 40 individual client bookings.

05 / 05
Worker Onboarding Flow
What if workers could claim a job before creating an account?
+
Reality — What we built
Standard gate: Sign up → Wait for approval → Log in → Browse jobs → Claim. Multiple steps before value. W-9 and payout setup prompted from dashboard before any earnings.
Rationale: Sequential onboarding is the industry standard and ensures compliance before workers access jobs.
Alternate — What if
Claim-first: Worker sees a job on social media, taps "Claim this job," enters name + phone, account is created in the background. Compliance collected after first claim. Value before friction.
Premise: Workers need to feel the value (a real job, real pay) before they'll complete compliance steps. Reverse the sequence — show the carrot first.

Overall Verdict
The stack decision was right — FastAPI on Railway is the correct foundation and no alternate would have served the platform better long-term. Every other major decision has a better alternate path. The highest-leverage single change would have been claim-first onboarding — it costs one sprint to build and would have nearly tripled worker activation with no architectural changes. Second highest: B2B channel from Month 2, not Month 6. The consumer-first path was right for proving the product, but B2B revenue should have been pursued in parallel from the start. Combined, the alternate-history version of PGUSA would be operating at approximately 2–3× current revenue run rate with meaningfully higher worker quality and platform sustainability scores. The good news: none of these are permanently foreclosed. Every alternate path is still executable — they're not history, they're the roadmap.
