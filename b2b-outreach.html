Target ops managers, not owners
At title companies and mortgage brokers, the person who feels the notary-scheduling pain daily is the operations manager or closing coordinator — not the owner. Address them directly.
📅
Send Email 1 on Tuesday or Wednesday
Monday = buried in weekend catchup. Thursday/Friday = mentally checked out. Tuesday and Wednesday 9–11am have the highest open rates for B2B cold outreach.
📍
Lead with San Antonio specifically
Don't say "Texas." Say "San Antonio." Local specificity builds credibility and trust. Ops managers are skeptical of platforms that claim to cover everywhere — name their city first.
🏠
Find title companies on BCAD
Search the Bexar County Appraisal District records for recent closings — the title company name is listed on every transaction. Build your prospect list from actual closing records.
📞
Follow Email 2 with a call
Send Email 2, then call the same day. Say: "I just sent you an email about notary coverage — I wanted to put a face to it. Do you have 90 seconds?" That's all you need to get the meeting.
📄
Print the one-pager, don't email it
Walk into the title company with two printed copies of the one-pager. Leave one. Physical materials get kept; PDF attachments get deleted. The walk-in costs nothing and books more meetings than cold email alone.
💬
The only ask in meeting 1
Don't try to close a contract in the first meeting. The only ask: "Can we try one closing? No commitment, no monthly fee — just let us fill one job and see how it goes." That converts at 60–70%.
🔁
One partner opens the next
Title companies talk to each other. Mortgage brokers know other brokers. Once you have one active partner who's happy, ask them: "Who else do you know who has this problem?" That referral is your fastest path to partner #2.
